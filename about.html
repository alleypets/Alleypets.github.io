About Alley Cats Allies

Dedicated to the welfare of animals since 2010

Our Story

How Alley Cats Allies came to be

Alley Cats Allies was founded in 2010 by a group of passionate animal lovers who saw the urgent need for a dedicated animal rescue in our community. What started as a small operation run out of a garage has grown into one of the region's most respected animal welfare organizations.

Our founder, Sarah Johnson, was a veterinary technician who frequently encountered stray and abandoned animals that had nowhere to go. Determined to make a difference, she gathered a team of like-minded volunteers and established Alley Cats Allies with a mission to rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome animals in need.

In our first year, we rescued 50 animals. Today, we rescue over 500 animals annually and have found loving homes for more than 4,500 cats and dogs since our founding.

Our Facility

Where animals find safety, care, and love

Our 5,000 square foot facility provides a safe and comfortable temporary home for up to 100 animals at a time. Our shelter includes:

  • Spacious individual kennels for dogs
  • Free-roam cat rooms with plenty of climbing and play spaces
  • On-site veterinary clinic for routine and emergency care
  • Outdoor play areas and walking trails
  • Training and socialization rooms
  • Meet-and-greet spaces for potential adopters

We've designed our shelter to minimize stress and provide enrichment for all animals in our care, creating an environment where they can heal and thrive while waiting for their forever homes.
